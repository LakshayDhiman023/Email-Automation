MAILFLOW — PRODUCT VISION
=========================================================================
The $0, self-hosted email outreach platform with a human in the loop.
Version: 0.1 (working product) · Vision doc v1 · 2026-07-02

-------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. MISSION
-------------------------------------------------------------------------
Give one person the outreach power of a paid sales/recruiting stack —
without the subscription, without handing a contact list to a SaaS vendor,
and without ever sending an email a human didn't approve.

Clone it. Plug in your own Supabase and Gmail. Deploy for free. It emails
AS YOU, from YOUR account, on YOUR schedule, with YOUR data staying yours.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. THE PROBLEM
-------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Outreach tools (Lemlist, Instantly, Smartlead, Gem, hireEZ) are built
  for teams and priced like it ($30–$300+/mo). A student, job-seeker, or
  indie founder needs 20 thoughtful emails a week, not 10,000 blasts.
- Those tools hold your contact graph on their servers.
- Full automation is how people get their Gmail suspended and their
  reputation burned: no cap, no suppression hygiene, no review step.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. PRINCIPLES (what makes this different)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------
P1. FREE FOREVER FOR ONE USER — every dependency has a $0 tier
    (Supabase, Gmail API, free web hosts, cron-job.org).
P2. SELF-HOSTED & OWNED — your DB, your OAuth app, your tokens.
    Single-tenant by design; "multi-user" = another clone.
P3. HUMAN-IN-THE-LOOP — nothing sends without explicit approval.
    pending_approval -> approved -> sent is the heart of the product.
P4. DELIVERABILITY IS SACRED — daily cap, suppression list, cooldowns,
    bounce detection, send windows in the recipient's working hours.
P5. RESPECT THE CHANNEL — no scraping-and-spraying, no ToS-violating
    integrations (LinkedIn/X/Instagram DM automation: evaluated, REJECTED).
P6. LEARNING-GRADE CODE — readable FastAPI + React, real tests, real
    migrations; the repo doubles as a portfolio/teaching artifact.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. WHERE WE ARE (v0.1 — shipped)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------
[x] Dynamic templates: any {variable}; compose form auto-generates inputs
[x] Approval pipeline with edit-before-send, cancel, thread close
[x] Scheduling: configurable timezone, send windows, working days,
    country holidays; follow-ups after N working days
[x] Reply detection + manual labeling (positive/negative/OOO with
    return-date rescheduling); bounce -> auto-suppression
[x] Safety rails: 40/day rolling cap, 30-day cooldown (force-overridable),
    hard suppression list, retry with audit trail, orphan recovery
[x] Works on sleeping free hosts: external cron drives POST /tasks/run
[x] Settings-driven identity ({signature}, sender name), per-template
    resume attachment, CSV export, metrics (reply/bounce rates)
[x] OWASP-hardened API (auth throttle, security headers, header-injection
    guards, fail-closed export), 29 tests, CI
[x] Dropbox-language UI: electric blue on white, pill buttons, profile
    widget

-------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. ROADMAP
-------------------------------------------------------------------------
NEAR (v0.2 — polish the single-player loop)
- One-command setup: script that provisions schema, seeds templates,
  checks Gmail auth ("clone -> running in 15 minutes")
- Published Google OAuth app guide (stop 7-day token expiry)
- Follow-up sequences: N steps per thread (today: one follow-up max)
- Inline reply viewing in the dashboard (today: snippet only)

MID (v0.3 — the "wow" layer, still $0)
- AI reply drafting via local Ollama: draft a response to a positive
  reply in your voice — still human-approved before sending (P3)
- Template analytics: reply rate per template, A/B compare
- Import contacts from CSV with per-row variable mapping
- Digest notifications (daily email summary of replies/pending)

LONG (v1.0 — a platform others build on)
- Pluggable "campaign packs": shareable template+sequence bundles
  (job hunt, fundraising, user research, link building)
- Provider abstraction: Outlook/SMTP alongside Gmail
- Optional lightweight login so a deployment can safely live on a
  public URL (replaces the baked-in token trade-off)
- Community: one-click deploy buttons (Render/Railway/Fly), docs site

NON-GOALS (deliberate)
- LinkedIn/X/Instagram DM automation (ToS-prohibited; harms P5)
- Multi-tenant SaaS, billing, teams
- Open/click tracking pixels (creepy; unreliable; against P5)
- Volume features: no 1,000-email blasts, ever (P4)

-------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE
-------------------------------------------------------------------------
- A stranger clones the repo and sends their first approved email within
  15 minutes, having paid nothing.
- Reply rate stays a first-class metric; sent volume never is.
- Zero Gmail suspensions among users following the defaults.
- The codebase is cited/forked as a reference FastAPI+React project.
